Nebula Gallery

A nebula is basically a giant cloud of gas found in space. The gas in question is mostly hydrogen mixed with helium, dust, other ionized gases and whatever else might be floating around way out there.

My Top Four Nebulas

The interaction of two doomed stars has created this spectacular ring adorned with bright clumps of gas — a diamond necklace of cosmic proportions.

The Ring Nebula is a planetary nebula in northern constellation of Lyra. It was formed when a star expels luminous envelope of ionized gas into the surrounding space.

The Eagle Nebula is a young open cluster of stars in the constellation Serpens, discovered by Jean-Philippe de Cheseaux in 1745–46

The Crab Nebula is a supernova remnant and pulsar wind nebula in the constellation of Taurus. The name comes from William Parsons, who observed the object in 1842.

Diffuse nebulas are called planetary nebula, they created when a star reaches its final evolutionary state - the changes from a red giant to a white dwarf. Although massive stars may instead become neutron stars, over 97% of the stars in our Milky Way will eventually end their lives as a white dwarf, including the Sun.

- James Miller, "10 Most Spectacular Nebulae in the Universe"
